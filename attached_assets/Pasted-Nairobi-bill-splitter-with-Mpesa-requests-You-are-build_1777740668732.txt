Nairobi bill splitter with Mpesa requests


You are building a full production-grade group bill splitting and Mpesa payment request tool designed specifically for the Kenyan market. Read the full brief, expand the vision, present ~20 substantial phases, wait for "next."
The pain:
Every group dinner, group trip, shared apartment utility bill, and office lunch collection in Nairobi ends with the same problem. One person pays. Then they spend the next three days sending WhatsApp reminders to recover their money from six friends who all say "nitakupigia" and then go quiet. The social friction is real — friendships are strained over Ksh 800. The tools that exist globally (Splitwise, Venmo) don't integrate with Mpesa and have zero adoption in Kenya. The solution is a tool that does the split calculation and then actually sends each person an Mpesa STK push request — so paying back isn't a favour, it's a button you press.
What you are building:
A mobile-first web app. The person who paid opens it, enters the total amount, adds the names and Mpesa numbers of everyone involved, optionally itemises the bill (or splits equally), and hits send. Each person immediately gets an Mpesa STK push for their share — their phone prompts them to pay, they enter their PIN, done. The payer sees in real time who has paid and who hasn't. No app download. Works on any phone. The viral loop is built in — every group that uses it once uses it again, and the non-payer who gets the STK push becomes a user when they're the payer next time.
Critical nuances:
Mpesa STK push requires the recipient's phone number to be registered on Mpesa — handle gracefully when it isn't. The split must handle unequal splits (I had the steak and the cocktail, you had the salad) — itemised splitting by person is important. The tool must be genuinely instant — from "open app" to "requests sent" in under 2 minutes. There should be a persistent record — "trips" or "events" that you can refer back to. The monetisation is careful: a 1% fee on amounts processed, or a Ksh 10 flat fee per request round, or a premium tier with group history and analytics. The core product must feel free or near-free or people won't use it for small bills.
Expand this. Think about: a shared trip expense tracker (not just one bill but accumulating expenses across a weekend trip); a flatmate recurring bills tracker (split rent, electricity, WiFi automatically every month); a WhatsApp bot version where you forward the bill photo and it parses and splits automatically; a social feed showing recent group activities (opt-in, like Venmo's social layer); and a business version for small offices splitting team lunch orders.
Hosting: Next.js on Vercel with edge functions for STK push initiation. Mpesa Daraja on a Railway backend. Extremely lightweight infrastructure — this should cost almost nothing to run. PWA for home screen installation on Android.
